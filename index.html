About Me

Hello everyone, I’m Iyanna Moné! I am from Richmond, Virginia although I spent a lot of my childhood in the tidewater area. I’m the CEO and Founder of Iyanna Moné Essentials where I offer everyday glam essentials to women and girls. I’m also the current Brand Ambassador for a non-profit called Girls For A Change where I advocate for girls of color in my community.

Currently a 17-year-old senior in highschool. I’ve attended Virginia Virtual Academy going on 3 years now. I’m currently a part of National Honors Society (NHS) and the Ambassador for National Society of High School Scholars (NSHSS). Throughout my school experience I’ve attended public school(8 years), private school(3 years), and online school(3 years). Switching learning environments allowed me to adjust to all learning styles hybrid, online, and in person. I’ve changed school environments for many reasons, one being the racial discrimation I’ve faced from teachers and students. I decided to attend online because it allowed me to have a lot more flexibility when it came to running Iyanna Moné Essentials.

After high school I’m attending Full Sail University majoring in Film Production. Moving to the “sunshine state” will allow me to create a life-long network in this industry. Since a young age, I've always had a love for visual arts, storytelling, and content creation. With film I hope to create generational impact and produce positive change by giving more women of color opportunities on and off screen and creating a better narrative surrounded by them!

After graduating from Full Sail University I have hopes of going straight into my career as a Filmmaker. For a few years I hope to work for a production company script-writing, directing, editing, producing, and costume designing for feature films. After creating a foundation for my film career I have plans of branching off and starting my own production company. My goal with film is to bring more people of color on and off screen. Growing seeing other ethnic groups creating narratives surrounded by black people pushed me to begin a career in film. I believe “black people should be telling black stories”. I would glance at film credits and realize they were being created by other ethnicities. With film I hope to travel the world telling impactful narratives.

I would be a great asset to this industry because I have a diverse background as an entrepreneur, content creator, web designer, social leader, journalist, seamstress, and graphic designer. With all that being said I hope to create films that have a generational impact and produce a positive change!
